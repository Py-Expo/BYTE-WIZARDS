Engineering-Courses

  • Civil Engineering:

    Civil Engineering deals with the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure. It includes sub-disciplines such as structural engineering, geotechnical engineering, transportation engineering, and environmental engineering.

  • Mechanical Engineering:

    Mechanical Engineering involves the design, analysis, and manufacturing of mechanical systems. Areas of study include thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials science, and mechanical design.

  • Electrical Engineering:

    Electrical Engineering focuses on the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. It covers areas like digital systems, control systems, power systems, and telecommunications.

  • Electronics and Communication Engineering:

    Electronics and Communication Engineering deals with the design and development of electronic circuits, communication systems, and digital signal processing.

  • Computer Science and Engineering:

    Computer Science and Engineering combines principles of computer science with engineering techniques. It encompasses software development, computer architecture, algorithms, artificial intelligence, and data science.

  • AI&DS (Artificial Intelligence and Data Science):

    Artificial Intelligence and Data Science (AI&DS) is used to tackle complex real-world problems(web search, speech recognition, and machine translation) that span across various practices of engineering with rigorous mathematical tools.

  • CS&BS (Computer Science and Business Systems):

    Computer Science and Business Systems (CSBS) is primarily concerned with computing, algorithm analysis, programming languages, programme design, software engineering, computer hardware, computer networks, and problem resolution.

  • Chemical Engineering:

    Chemical Engineering involves the application of chemistry and physics principles to design and optimize processes for large-scale manufacturing. It includes areas like chemical processes, materials, and environmental engineering.

  • Biotechnology Engineering:

    Biotechnology Engineering applies biological principles to develop technologies and products. It includes genetic engineering, bioinformatics, and applications in healthcare, agriculture, and environmental management.

  • Aerospace Engineering:

    Aerospace Engineering focuses on the design, development, and testing of aircraft and spacecraft. It includes aerodynamics, propulsion systems, and aerospace structures.

  • Environmental Engineering:

    Environmental Engineering addresses the challenges of environmental protection and sustainable development. It involves water treatment, air pollution control, waste management, and environmental impact assessment.

  • Petroleum Engineering:

    Petroleum Engineering is concerned with the exploration, extraction, and processing of oil and gas. It includes reservoir engineering, drilling techniques, and petroleum refining.

  • Industrial Engineering:

    Industrial Engineering optimizes processes and systems in various industries. It involves production planning, quality control, supply chain management, and efficiency improvement.

  • Biomedical Engineering:

    Biomedical Engineering combines engineering principles with medical and biological sciences. It includes the development of medical devices, imaging systems, and healthcare technologies.

Arts-Courses

  • Literature:

    Literature courses explore written works, including novels, poetry, plays, and essays. Students analyze literary techniques, themes, and historical contexts.

  • Fine Arts:

    Fine Arts courses encompass visual arts such as drawing, painting, sculpture, and printmaking. Students learn artistic techniques and explore creativity.

  • Performing Arts:

    Performing Arts include theater, dance, and music courses. Students engage in acting, choreography, or musical performance, gaining practical experience in the arts.

  • History of Art:

    History of Art courses trace the development of visual arts over time, examining styles, movements, and the impact of art on cultures.

  • Philosophy of Art:

    Philosophy of Art explores theoretical aspects of art, addressing questions about beauty, meaning, and the nature of artistic expression.

  • Film Studies:

    Film Studies courses analyze the art and history of cinema. Students examine film techniques, genres, and cultural impact.

  • Photography:

    Photography courses teach the principles of capturing and editing images. Students learn about composition, lighting, and digital editing.

  • Graphic Design:

    Graphic Design courses focus on creating visual content for communication purposes. Students learn design principles, typography, and digital tools.

  • Cultural Studies:

    Cultural Studies courses examine the role of arts in shaping and reflecting cultures. Students analyze the intersections of art, society, and identity.

  • Creative Writing:

    Creative Writing courses cover fiction, non-fiction, and poetry. Students develop their writing skills and explore various genres.

  • Art History:

    Art History courses delve into the study of artworks from different periods, regions, and cultures. Students learn to analyze and interpret visual art.

  • Sociology of Art:

    Sociology of Art explores the social aspects of artistic production, consumption, and reception. Students examine the role of art in society.

  • Musicology:

    Musicology courses focus on the study of music history, theory, and cultural contexts. Students explore different genres and musical traditions.

  • Drama and Theatre Arts:

    Drama and Theatre Arts courses cover theatrical production, acting techniques, and the history of drama. Students may participate in stage performances.

  • Fashion Design:

    Fashion Design courses teach the principles of clothing design, fabric selection, and fashion trends. Students may create their own designs.

MBBS-Courses

  • MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery):

    MBBS is the foundational course for medical education. It covers a broad range of subjects including anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pathology, pharmacology, and clinical medicine. The course typically spans around 5-6 years and includes clinical rotations.

  • MD (Doctor of Medicine):

    MD is a postgraduate degree that allows medical graduates to specialize in a specific area such as internal medicine, surgery, pediatrics, etc. The duration varies by country but generally takes 2-3 years.

  • MS (Master of Surgery):

    Similar to MD, MS is a postgraduate surgical degree. It allows medical graduates to specialize in surgical fields such as general surgery, orthopedic surgery, or neurosurgery.

  • Dental Courses (BDS and DDS):

    BDS (Bachelor of Dental Surgery) and DDS (Doctor of Dental Surgery) are undergraduate and postgraduate courses, respectively, focusing on dentistry. Dentists diagnose and treat oral health issues.

  • Nursing Courses (BSc Nursing, MSc Nursing):

    Nursing courses prepare individuals for a career in patient care. BSc Nursing is an undergraduate program, while MSc Nursing is a postgraduate program that allows specialization in areas like pediatric nursing or psychiatric nursing.

  • Pharmacy Courses (BPharm, PharmD):

    BPharm is an undergraduate course in pharmacy, while PharmD is a postgraduate doctorate program. Pharmacists play a crucial role in dispensing medications and advising patients on their use.

  • Physiotherapy Courses (BPT, MPT):

    Bachelor of Physiotherapy (BPT) is an undergraduate course that trains individuals in physical therapy. Master of Physiotherapy (MPT) is a postgraduate program allowing specialization in areas like orthopedics or neurology.

  • Occupational Therapy Courses (BOT, MOT):

    Bachelor of Occupational Therapy (BOT) and Master of Occupational Therapy (MOT) train individuals to help patients develop or recover daily living and work skills.

  • Veterinary Science (BVSc & AH, MVSc):

    BVSc & AH (Bachelor of Veterinary Science and Animal Husbandry) is an undergraduate course in veterinary science. MVSc (Master of Veterinary Science) is a postgraduate program that allows specialization.

  • Public Health Courses (MPH, PhD in Public Health):

    Master of Public Health (MPH) is a postgraduate course focusing on public health issues. It involves epidemiology, health policy, and community health. PhD in Public Health is a doctoral program for advanced research.

  • Optometry (B.Optom, M.Optom):

    Bachelor of Optometry (B.Optom) and Master of Optometry (M.Optom) train individuals to provide eye care, including vision testing and prescribing corrective lenses.

  • Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT, MMLT):

    Bachelor of Medical Laboratory Technology (BMLT) and Master of Medical Laboratory Technology (MMLT) involve the study of laboratory techniques for diagnosis and research.
